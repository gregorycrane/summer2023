<note n="7.215.2">It is very difficult to retain the υἱὸν of the MSS. in
the sense of “descendant”. I have adopted the correc- tion υἱωνὸν (though ὑϊδοῦν would be equally possible) on
the assumption that Lucian (who reserves the name Doris
for the Nereid, cf. p. 179 note) has replaced Doris, the traditional
mother of Thetis, by the General goddess of the sea,
Thalatta, who first appears thus in Bion, I, 13 and Meleager,
<hi rend="italic">A.P</hi>. V, 180. Lucian may be thinking of works of art, as
Pausanias, 2.1.7 mentions statues of Thalatta at Corinth,
while Philostratus, <hi rend="italic">Imag</hi>. II, 16 also describes Thalattai at
Corinth.</note>
